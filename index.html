About PESA

PESA (Planet Earth’s Student Army) is a Colorado public benefit corporation created to provide global collaboration tools and infrastructure to students, instructors, climate experts, multinational corporations, governments, nonprofits, and climate change volunteers of any age at no cost. Initially launching in the U.S and Vietnam on August 22, PESA’s app (on Apple’s App Store) will be available in all 196 countries. Learning from organizations like the Peace Corp, Red Cross, Salvation Army, and The Duke of Edinburgh’s Award program PESA combines successful aspects of older organizations with significant advances in technology allowing volunteers to create, collaborate, share knowledge and implement climate solutions with others across the globe.

Why PESA

Last year’s climate summit in Glasgow discussed several areas of progress and areas needing improvement. Two areas that stood out were: 1) the lack of global collaboration between governments, multi-national corporations, and NGOs and 2) the need to speed up progress. PESA’s founders understood that speeding up global change would require the planet’s youth to unite and work hand in hand with governments, scientist, corporations, NGOs, and large corporations to accelerate climate change. In short, it will take a student army to meet 2030 and 2050 goals. PESA was built to provide the collaboration, solution plans, knowledge-base, and project management tools to support ‘shovel-ready’ projects to ensure future generations can live as we have.
